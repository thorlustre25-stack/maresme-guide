💡 Coastal Cycling Tips

  • Seafront bike path: There's a bike lane along Calella's seafront promenade - flat and scenic
  • Train + bike: You can take bikes on the R1 train (off-peak hours) - cycle one way, train back
  • Barcelona ride: It's ~50km from Calella to Barcelona along the coast - doable for fit cyclists
  • Best time: Spring and early autumn - summer can be very hot

Hiking & Nature

Mountains, forests & 500km of trails behind the beaches

15,000 Hectares

Parc Natural del Montnegre i el Corredor

A natural park right behind the coast with 500km of marked trails. Mediterranean forests, cork trees, ancient churches, and amazing sea views. Most tourists never know it exists.

What You'll Find

  • Cork oak and Mediterranean forests
  • Dolmens and Iberian settlement ruins
  • Medieval hermitages and chapels
  • Wildlife: foxes, squirrels, eagles, wild boar
  • Views of the coast from above

Popular Routes

Route Distance Difficulty

Best Time

Spring (wildflowers) and autumn (cooler). Avoid midday in summer - it gets hot!

Cycling Routes

Maresme is certified as a cycling tourism destination. Over 1,000km of routes from flat coastal paths to challenging mountain climbs.

Route Types

  • Coastal flat: Calella seafront path, town-to-town along the coast
  • Mountain bike: Trails in Montnegre Natural Park
  • Road cycling: Loops into the hills with sea views
  • Barcelona ride: ~50km coastal route to the city
Official Cycling Info Routes on Bikemap

💡 Hiking Tips

  • Bring water - There are few facilities on the trails
  • Download maps offline - Signal can be patchy in the hills
  • Wear proper shoes - Trails can be rocky
  • Start early in summer - It gets hot by midday
  • Pick up a map - Tourist offices have free hiking maps
